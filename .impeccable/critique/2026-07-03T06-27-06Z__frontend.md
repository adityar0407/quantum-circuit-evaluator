#### Overall Impression
The app has the beginnings of a credible research tool, but it still feels like a template being forced into a serious workflow. The biggest opportunity is to stop treating every subsection like a separate “feature card” and instead make the task flow feel like one calm instrument.

#### What's Working
- The color direction is materially better than the earlier dark/glassy shell. The warm neutrals and restrained green are closer to the stated research-grade tone.
- Stage gating is conceptually correct. Requiring validation before architecture work is the right product behavior.
- The copy is more domain-specific than a generic AI SaaS template. Terms like architecture-aware compilation, routing pressure, and estimator assumptions are appropriate for the audience.

#### Priority Issues
- **[P1] The interface still has no settled component hierarchy**
  - **Why it matters**: Researchers need to trust the surface immediately. Right now the landing hero, section pills, sidebar workflow, panel headers, and boxed metric cards all compete for attention, which makes the product feel assembled instead of deliberate.
  - **Fix**: Reduce the number of visual emphasis layers. Pick one hierarchy system for section headers, one for stage navigation, and one for supportive metadata. Remove repeated eyebrow/pill framing where it adds no decision value.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable distill`
- **[P1] Stage 1 and Stage 2 still over-prioritize chrome over the primary task**
  - **Why it matters**: The user’s real job is to load a circuit, validate it, inspect the target, and move on. Instead, the interface spends too much space on shells, labels, and side framing before the core controls earn focus.
  - **Fix**: Rebalance the stage layouts so the editor and architecture controls dominate first, while workflow navigation and explanatory framing step back.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout`
- **[P1] Form-control robustness is still below product quality**
  - **Why it matters**: The architecture page is a high-trust form surface. Truncated values, custom chevron drift, inconsistent box widths, and fragile single-line behavior immediately erode confidence.
  - **Fix**: Normalize select/input shells, reserve width for long target labels, and define breakpoint behavior for the architecture grid as a system instead of case-by-case patches.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish`
- **[P2] Empty, loading, and pending states are technically present but emotionally dead**
  - **Why it matters**: A research tool needs to reassure users that the workflow is ready, waiting, or incomplete for a reason. “Loading” and large blank cards make the product feel unfinished rather than precise.
  - **Fix**: Replace generic placeholders with explicit waiting states that explain what action unlocks the next surface and what data will appear there.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable clarify`
- **[P2] The product tone is split between research instrument and landing-page marketing**
  - **Why it matters**: The user asked for serious, credible, non-generic UI. The homepage still opens with a large promotional hero structure that belongs to product marketing more than a local expert tool.
  - **Fix**: Either compress the landing surface into a terse workspace handoff, or make it an actual documentation-oriented arrival screen instead of a pseudo-marketing hero.
  - **Suggested command**: `$impeccable shape`

#### Persona Red Flags
- **Mira (Quantum researcher comparing hardware assumptions)**: The architecture stage asks her to trust the tool while showing brittle field containers and large empty preview regions. That makes the hardware model feel less reliable than the underlying domain requires.
- **Alex (Power User)**: The workflow is visually heavy for a repetitive expert task. The sidebar, repeated section framing, and oversized stage headers consume space that should go to denser circuit and target work.
- **Jordan (First-Timer)**: The staged path is understandable, but the interface still asks them to parse too many boxes and labels before they know where to look. The tool teaches, but not efficiently.

#### Minor Observations
- The top toolbar still reads slightly too much like a brochure nav for a local research workspace.
- Uppercase section pills are overused; one deliberate label system would be enough.
- The architecture preview side is still too visually empty when no target graph is loaded.
- Buttons are closer to a coherent system, but secondary and disabled states still feel under-tuned.
