#### Overall Impression

The tool workspace is directionally better than the landing experience: it is explicit, segmented, and readable. The main problem is trust. Right now the app feels like a generic product shell adapted to quantum research rather than a UI designed from the workflow outward. The single biggest opportunity is to strip away generic landing/product theatrics and make the primary research flow feel more exact, more domain-specific, and more confidently hierarchical.

#### What's Working

- The staged workflow is legible. Users can understand the intended order of work without much explanation.
- The Terra palette is directionally correct for this audience. It avoids the usual neon or sci-fi defaults and moves the product toward a warmer, more grounded tone.
- The core tool surfaces already separate editing, architecture setup, estimation, and results clearly enough to support iterative research work.

#### Priority Issues

- **[P1] What**: Trust erosion from generic landing-page theater
  **Why it matters**: Researchers and compiler engineers are skeptical audiences. Generic hero copy, fake-looking metric strips, and broad claims make the product feel less rigorous before they even reach the tool.
  **Fix**: Remove or radically tighten the landing claims, eliminate decorative metrics that a serious user would not trust, and make the opening surface more domain-specific and evidence-driven.
  **Suggested command**: `$impeccable critique frontend`

- **[P1] What**: Structural mismatch between palette and layout language
  **Why it matters**: The warm Terra colors imply a grounded, research-grade tool, but the current layout still feels like a dark SaaS shell translated into cream. That disconnect makes the UI feel cosmetically changed rather than intentionally designed.
  **Fix**: Rework page composition, panel emphasis, and section rhythm so the structure matches the calmer palette instead of fighting it.
  **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout frontend`

- **[P1] What**: Weak primary-task hierarchy in Stage 1
  **Why it matters**: Import, validate, proceed, reset, stage navigation, and top-level nav are all visible too early. That dilutes the main path and increases decision load at the most important entry step.
  **Fix**: Tighten the first-step hierarchy so the user sees one obvious next move, with progression visually gated by circuit validity and readiness.
  **Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish frontend`

- **[P2] What**: Over-boxing and repeated panel treatment
  **Why it matters**: Nearly every information block uses the same bordered rectangle treatment, which flattens emphasis and makes the product feel templated.
  **Fix**: Reduce repeated card treatment, vary containment levels, and create stronger relationships between primary, secondary, and tertiary content.
  **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout frontend`

- **[P2] What**: Typography and voice are not yet carrying the brand
  **Why it matters**: The current mono-label-heavy system and generic heading rhythm do not yet communicate a research-grade, credible, and slightly warm product voice.
  **Fix**: Rebalance label density, heading cadence, and copy tone so the interface sounds and reads like a tool for compiler engineers rather than a generic app shell.
  **Suggested command**: `$impeccable typeset frontend`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Quantum Computing Researcher**: The landing-page claims and broad metrics create immediate skepticism. This user will question whether the product is rigorous before they trust any simulation output.

**Compiler Engineer**: The workspace is understandable, but progression hierarchy is too loose. Seeing “Proceed” adjacent to validation before readiness is fully established makes the flow feel less exact than the domain requires.

**Hardware Team Evaluator**: The UI is warm but not yet fully credible. Hardware specificity is not foregrounded strongly enough in the opening experience, so the tool reads as more generic than specialized.

#### Minor Observations

- The active stage styling is serviceable but not strong enough to anchor progress.
- The footer status indicator is too easy to miss to act as meaningful system feedback.
- The tool view is more mature than the landing view; the product currently feels split between two design ages.
- The empty-state copy is reasonably clear, but visually it does not teach or reassure enough.
