# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Researchers, compiler engineers, and hardware teams working in quantum computing. They use this tool while exploring how real circuits behave under different compilation paths, hardware architectures, and error-correction assumptions. Their workflow is analytical and comparative: they need to inspect constraints, test custom hardware profiles, and understand the resource implications of running a circuit on a specific target.

## Product Purpose
This product exists to make quantum circuit evaluation more concrete before hardware execution. It helps teams simulate and understand the cost of running algorithms against specific hardware profiles, compilers, and error-correction models, while remaining flexible enough to accept custom hardware specifications. Success looks like a tool that supports credible research decisions, exposes tradeoffs clearly, and makes comparative analysis faster and easier to trust.

## Brand Personality
Curious, clinical, and credible, with enough warmth to avoid feeling sterile. The product should feel research-grade and precise without turning into generic sci-fi or AI-product theater. It should communicate expertise through clarity and rigor rather than decoration.

## Anti-references
Do not make this look like a generic AI-generated app or a flashy VC-backed AI product. Avoid robotic futurism, empty "techy" decoration, glassy gradients, neon accents, and ornamental interface chrome that does not support the research workflow. This is a serious quantum computing research tool and should feel grounded, legible, and trustworthy.

## Design Principles
- Put analytical clarity ahead of visual novelty.
- Make credibility come from precision, consistency, and readable comparisons.
- Keep the interface warm enough to feel human, but disciplined enough to support expert work.
- Let customization and hardware specificity feel first-class, not bolted on.
- Favor calm, explicit surfaces that help users reason about tradeoffs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast across the application, including form states, charts, and status messages. Preserve clear focus states, keyboard accessibility, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Use color as reinforcement rather than the only signal for status, warnings, or results.
